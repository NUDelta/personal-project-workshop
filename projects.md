### 🎶Build-Your-Own Spotify Wrapped

*Note: due to recent restrictions on the Spotify API, you must have a premium subscription to use the Spotify API. If you use another platform or don’t have Spotify premium, you can look into APIs for other applications that could achieve the same functionality.*

At the end of every year, Spotify tells you a bit about your listening history during the past 11 months. But what if you could know more about your listening habits during the year, instead of only once at the end? Or what if you could explore other statistics that you’ve always wanted to know, but Spotify didn’t tell you? In this project, you will make your own Spotify Wrapped by using the data from the Spotify API to generate statistics about your own Spotify usage. How you want to display this data to the user is up to you. We encourage you to display it in the terminal to start, but you could consider building a frontend for your application, too\!

You can find information about the Spotify API [here](https://developer.spotify.com/documentation/web-api) 

### 📨Machine Learning Classifier for Handwritten Numbers

The US Postal Service relies on ZIP codes to help classify its mail and ensure timely delivery of millions of pieces of mail each day. This is no easy task – people typically handwrite the addresses on their mail, and not everyone’s handwriting is always easy to read. You are a software developer for USPS, and your job is to write a machine learning classifier for handwritten digits so the USPS can quickly and automatically identify ZIP codes when mail is scanned in. Your project will need to take in a 28x28 image of a single digit and output what number it actually is; we encourage you to build this interaction in the terminal to start.

Training data for your machine learning classifier can be found in the MNIST dataset, which is available through the [scikitlearn website](https://scikit-learn.org/stable/auto_examples/classification/plot_digits_classification.html) . scikitlearn is a python package often used for machine learning tasks. To start, we encourage you to build an understanding of, then try to implement, the Naive Bayes classifier algorithm, which classifies these digits based on probability from prior examples, rather than human-written rules.

### ♠️Your Favorite Card Game

What is your favorite card game, or a favorite card game from your childhood? While nothing beats sitting around a table and playing it with friends and family, we challenge you to turn your favorite card game into digital form. We encourage you to build the interactions in the terminal to start, but later on you could build a visual interface for the game, too. You could make the game for multiple human users, or create a bot that could play your game against the user (or both).

### 🖼️Computational Art

Computational artists consider computation and programming to be another medium of expression, not a replacement of art itself. Computation enables new forms of expression or extends existing ones. One project that we propose is using computation to introduce randomness or patterns. One example of randomness is [Conway's Game of Life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conway%27s_Game_of_Life) (try it at [https://playgameoflife.com/](https://playgameoflife.com/)) and an example of patterns is the [Mandelbrot Set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandelbrot_set) (try it at [https://mandelbrot.site/](https://mandelbrot.site/)). Implement one of these, your own modification of them, or take inspiration from them for your own ideas.

You will need to have some way of displaying the art to the user. You will need to look into Python libraries to achieve this. One place to start is tkinter, but you can also look for other libraries that better suit your specific needs.

### 🎲Text-based Game

Before it was easy, or even possible, to create video games with fancy graphics, text-based adventure games were quite popular (see [Interactive Fiction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interactive_fiction)). They continue to inspire modern creations today. Think of them like a choose your own adventure book, but in interactive form. Create your own text-based adventure game, where the user traverses through a story by interacting with the terminal. You get to imagine the theme, the story, and the interactions – it could be a grand adventure or a relaxing cozy game.
